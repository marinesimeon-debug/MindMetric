Anxiety.

Global Data: 301.4 Million The most prevalent psychiatric condition worldwide. Defined by chronic HPA-axis activation and systemic cortisol elevation.
Neurobiological Impact Amygdala hyper-reactivity, prefrontal cortex thinning, and GABAergic dysregulation.
Psychologist CBT Expert

Depression.

Economic Load: $1 Trillion Impacting 280 million adults globally. The primary driver of lost productivity and global disability-adjusted life years (DALYs).
Clinical Indicators Persistent anhedonia, lethargy, circadian fragmentation, and executive dysfunction.

Addiction.

Cases: 40 Million+ The hijacking of the nucleus accumbens reward system through chronic dopamine flooding.
Addictologist Detox Unit

Schizophrenia.

Prevalence: 24 Million Thought fragmentation and perceptual distortion. Linked to excess Dopamine D2 activity in the mesolimbic pathway.
Critical Risk: Morbidity rates are significantly higher due to metabolic and cardiovascular neglect.

Bipolarity.

Global Statistics: 40 Million Oscillations between manic hyper-arousal and severe depressive episodes. High risk of systemic life destabilization.
Psychiatrist

PTSD.

Origin: Trauma Characterized by flashbacks, severe emotional numbing, and hyper-vigilance responses.
EMDR Therapist

Autism.

Prevalence: 1 in 100 Neurodevelopmental differences in social communication and sensory processing. Acknowledging neurological diversity.
Neurologist

ADHD.

Executive Dysfunction Impacts attention regulation and impulse control. Associated with frontostriatal circuit connectivity deltas.
Neuropsychologist

OCD.
