Anxiety Disorders

Quantitative Data: 301 Million Cases Research confirms anxiety as the leading psychiatric condition globally. 75% of clinical cases are identified before the age of 24.
Clinical Symptoms & Markers Persistent cognitive fog, somatization (tachycardia), and maladaptive avoidant behaviors.
Predictive Outcomes If untreated, chronic anxiety correlates with future cardiovascular dysfunction and social decoupling.

"Neurobiology of Uncertainty."

Pathways to recovery include:

Clinical Psychologist CBT Therapist
Reference: Harvard Medical School Research Library

Clinical Depression

Economic Impact: $1 Trillion Annual Loss MDD (Major Depressive Disorder) affects 280 million people, representing the leading cause of global disability adjusted life years.
Primary Symptoms Anhedonia (inability to feel pleasure), disrupted circadian rhythms, and executive function deficit.
Medical Referral Crucial for managing suicidal ideation and neurochemical imbalances.

Specialists Recommended:

Psychiatrist (MD) Psychotherapist Data: WHO Mental Health Gap Programme (mhGAP)

Anger & Impulse

Neurological Basis: Amygdala Overdrive Weakened synaptic connectivity between the prefrontal cortex (control) and the amygdala (emotion).
Clinical Indicators Intermittent Explosive Disorder (IED) markers: rapid heart rate, verbal aggression, and immediate remorse.
Long-term Risks Predicts high vulnerability to substance use disorders and interpersonal trauma.

Specialists & Intervention:

Neuropsychologist Anger Management Specialist Source: DSM-5-TR Clinical Guidelines

Bipolar Disorder

Demographics: 40 Million People A spectrum disorder characterized by extreme shifts in energy, mood, and activity levels.
Symptomatic Phases Mania (pressured speech, decreased need for sleep) alternating with severe depressive episodes.
Clinical Prognosis Requires lifelong management to prevent severe social and financial destabilization.

Mandatory Clinical Path:

Psychiatrist (Mood Specialist)

Combined pharmacotherapy and psychoeducation.

Source: International Society for Bipolar Disorders (ISBD)
