Anxiety Disorders

Definition: A cluster of disorders characterized by excessive fear, anxiety, and related behavioral disturbances.

Etiology & Pathophysiology
Rooted in the dysregulation of the HPA Axis (Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal) and hypersensitivity of the Amygdala. This results in an "alarm system" that remains active even in the absence of a threat, disrupting the balance of GABA and Glutamate neurotransmission.
Symptomatic Triad
1. Cognitive: Persistent rumination and catastrophic thinking.
2. Physiological: Tachycardia, dyspnea, and hypercortisolism.
3. Behavioral: Avoidance of triggers (maladaptive coping).

Major Depressive Disorder

Definition: A persistent state of low mood and aversion to activity that affects a person's thoughts, behavior, and physical well-being.

Neuro-Anatomical Markers
Long-term depression is associated with reduced hippocampal volume and decreased synaptic density in the Dorsolateral Prefrontal Cortex (DLPFC). This explains the "brain fog" and memory deficits reported by patients.
Bio-Chemical Deficit
Characterized by the Monoamine Hypothesis: a functional deficiency of Serotonin, Norepinephrine, and/or Dopamine in the synaptic cleft, leading to profound anhedonia (loss of pleasure).

Bipolar Spectrum

Definition: A brain disorder that causes unusual shifts in mood, energy, activity levels, and the ability to carry out day-to-day tasks.

Cyclic Dynamics
Divided into Bipolar I (at least one manic episode) and Bipolar II (hypomanic and depressive episodes). Manic phases involve "pressured speech" and a diminished need for sleep without fatigue.
Genetic Predisposition
Boasts a heritability rate of ~80%. Research focuses on calcium signaling genes (CACNA1C) and their role in neuronal excitability during mood transitions.

Schizophrenia

Definition: A complex, chronic mental health disorder characterized by disruptions in thought processes, perceptions, emotional responsiveness, and social interactions.

The Dopamine Hypothesis
Postulates that overactivity of dopamine in the mesolimbic pathway causes "Positive Symptoms" (hallucinations/delusions), while underactivity in the mesocortical pathway causes "Negative Symptoms" (apathy/alogia).
Cognitive Disorganization
Involves "Loose Associations" where thoughts are not logically connected, making communication difficult. Often accompanied by anosognosia (lack of insight into the illness).
