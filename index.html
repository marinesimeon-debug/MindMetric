Addiction & Reward

Quantitative Data: 35 Million Disorders NIDA estimates suggest 35 million people suffer from drug-use disorders. Digital and behavioral addictions are showing a 20% annual growth rate.
Clinical Symptoms & Signs Tolerance development, loss of impulse control, and neurochemical "hijacking" of the dopamine reward circuit.
Predictive Risks Unmanaged addiction correlates with permanent neuroplasticity alterations and high comorbid major depression.

"The Dopamine Hijack."

Clinical specialists involved:

Addictologist Psychiatrist (MD) CBT Specialist
Source: NIH / National Institute on Drug Abuse

Anxiety Disorders

Quantitative Data: 301 Million Cases Research confirms anxiety as the leading psychiatric condition globally. 75% of cases are identified before age 24.
Clinical Symptoms & Markers Persistent cognitive fog, somatization (tachycardia), and maladaptive avoidant behaviors.
Predictive Outcomes Chronic anxiety predicts future cardiovascular dysfunction and professional social decoupling.

"Neurobiology of Uncertainty."

Specialists for care:

Clinical Psychologist CBT Therapist
Reference: Harvard Medical School Research Library

Clinical Depression

Economic Impact: $1 Trillion Annual Loss MDD affects 280 million people, representing the leading cause of global disability adjusted life years (DALYs).
Primary Symptoms Anhedonia (inability to feel pleasure), disrupted circadian rhythms, and executive function deficit.
Medical Referral Crucial for managing suicidal ideation and restoring neurochemical synaptic balance.

Recommended Specialists:

Psychiatrist (MD) Psychotherapist Data: WHO Mental Health Gap Programme

Anger & Impulse

Neurological Basis: Amygdala Overdrive Weakened synaptic connectivity between the prefrontal cortex (control) and the amygdala (emotion).
Clinical Indicators Intermittent Explosive Disorder (IED) markers: rapid heart rate, verbal aggression, and post-crisis remorse.
Long-term Risks Predicts high vulnerability to substance use disorders and chronic interpersonal trauma.

Specialists & Intervention:

Neuropsychologist Behavioral Therapist Source: DSM-5-TR Clinical Guidelines

Bipolar Disorder

Demographics: 40 Million People A spectrum disorder characterized by extreme shifts in energy, mood, and cognitive activity levels.
Symptomatic Phases Mania (pressured speech, hyper-activity) alternating with severe clinical depressive episodes.
Clinical Prognosis Requires lifelong clinical management to prevent social and financial destabilization.

Mandatory Clinical Path:

Psychiatrist (Mood Specialist)

Focus: Pharmacotherapy and psychoeducation.

Source: International Society for Bipolar Disorders
