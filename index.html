Anxiety Disorders

Neuro-Biological Core: Axis HPA Dysregulation of the Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal (HPA) axis leads to sustained cortisol elevation, inhibiting neurogenesis in the hippocampus.
Comorbidity & Risk 70% of individuals with GAD (Generalized Anxiety Disorder) also meet the criteria for Major Depressive Disorder at some point in their life.
Symptomatic Spectrum Hyper-vigilance, somatic autonomic arousal (tremors, palpitations), and cognitive avoidance of perceived future threats.

Major Depression

Neuroplasticity & BDNF Severe cases show reduced levels of Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (BDNF), causing synaptic shrinkage in the prefrontal cortex.
The Circadian Disruption Depression is highly linked to early-morning awakening and REM sleep abnormalities (shorter REM latency).
Symptomatic Markers Psychomotor retardation, profound anhedonia, and the "cognitive triad" (self-criticism, hopelessness, and global pessimism).

Data Source: Lancet Psychiatry 2025 Study

Schizophrenia

Dopaminergic Hypothesis Excess dopamine in the mesolimbic pathway causes "positive" symptoms (delusions), while deficit in the mesocortical pathway causes "negative" symptoms (withdrawal).
Structural Changes Advanced MRI imaging shows lateral ventricle enlargement and thinning of the superior temporal gyrus.
Cognitive Deficits Often characterized by impaired "Executive Function" (difficulty planning, focusing, and using working memory).

Management Protocol:

Early intervention (FEP) reduces long-term hospitalization rates by 40%.

Bipolar Disorder

The Genetic Component Heritability is estimated at 80%, making it one of the most genetically-linked psychiatric conditions.
Cycle Variation Rapid cycling is defined by 4 or more mood episodes within a 12-month period.
Mania vs Hypomania Mania involves functional impairment and possible psychosis; hypomania is less severe but includes persistent elevated energy levels.
