Anxiety

The most common mental health disorder worldwide according to World Health Organization data.

Massive Impact: 301 Million Cases Affecting a growing portion of the youth population, particularly within academic environments.
Critical Onset Age: Before 24 Studies indicate that the majority of anxiety disorders solidify during the transition into adulthood.

"Anxiety is not just a passing fear; it is a systemic brain response to persistent uncertainty."

Clinical Depression

Deep clinical analysis based on Harvard Medical School research findings.

WHO Statistics: 280 Million Depression is the leading cause of disability worldwide, impacting global GDP by nearly $1 trillion annually.
Risk Factor: 1 in 2 People Comprehensive data synthesis suggests that the risk of developing a mood disorder during a lifetime is exceptionally high.

Anger & Impulse

Focusing on impulse control disorders and the management of chronic irritability.

Neurobiology: Direct correlation with prefrontal cortex regulation and amygdala responses.
Manifestation: Intermittent Explosive Disorder often affects young adults, impacting social and professional integration.

Data Alert:

Impulsivity is frequently the primary signal for more complex, undiagnosed psychological conditions.

Bipolar Disorder

Understanding the cyclical nature of mood regulation and neurotransmitter patterns.

Global Prevalence: 40 Million A complex disorder characterized by alternating manic and depressive episodes.
Diagnostic Research: Requires long-term data tracking for accurate clinical intervention.

"Bipolarity is a spectrum of energy regulation, not just mood swings."
