Anxiety Disorders

Mechanism: Amygdala Hyper-Reactivity Anxiety is driven by an overactive fear-processing center (Amygdala) and a failure of the Prefrontal Cortex to down-regulate the survival response.
Pathological Sub-types Includes Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD), Panic Disorder (PD), Social Anxiety (SAD), and Specific Phobias.
Prevalence: 1 in 13 People The most frequent mental disorder category globally. High correlation with gastrointestinal issues and sleep fragmentation.

Clinical Interventions

Focus on Cognitive Reframing and Exposure Therapy (ERP).

Major Depression (MDD)

Neurochemistry: The Monoamine Hypothesis A complex deficit in serotonin, norepinephrine, and dopamine transmission within the mesolimbic and cortical pathways.
Cognitive Markers: The Triad Negative views of the self, the world, and the future. Often accompanied by psychomotor retardation or agitation.
Mortality Risk Untreated MDD increases suicide risk by 20x compared to the general population. Leading cause of global DALYs.

Schizophrenia Spectrum

Positive vs Negative Symptoms Positive: Hallucinations and Delusions. Negative: Alogia (lack of speech), Avolition (lack of drive), and Affective Flattening.
Neuro-Anatomy Enlarged cerebral ventricles and decreased cortical thickness in the temporal and frontal lobes.
Recovery Model Early Intervention in Psychosis (EIP) services significantly improve long-term functional outcomes.

Therapeutic Strategy:

Combination of Antipsychotics and Social Integration Therapy.

Addictive Pathologies

The Dopamine Hijack Chronic substance use desensitizes the Nucleus Accumbens, requiring higher doses for a diminished pleasure response (tolerance).
Craving & Relapse Environmental cues trigger the dorsal striatum, leading to compulsive seeking behaviors regardless of negative consequences.

ADHD / TDAH

Executive Function Deficit Impairment in working memory, emotional regulation, and impulse control due to catecholamine dysregulation.
The Adult ADHD Rise Research highlights that 60% of childhood cases persist into adulthood, affecting career and relationship stability.
